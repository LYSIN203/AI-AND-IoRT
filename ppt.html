1. The Concept: IoRT

Definition:

The Internet of Robotic Things (IoRT) is an advanced concept where intelligent devices can monitor events, fuse sensor data, and decide a course of action to control physical objects.

Essentially, it adds "Embodied Intelligence" (Robots) to the standard IoT network, allowing digital systems to interact physically with the real world.

"IoRT introduces a new class of intelligent devices... fusing sensor data to determine a best course of action."

Ref: IEEE Access (Research)

2. Mechanism: Efficiency Loop

How does the system think? It follows a 3-step cycle:

1. Perception

Sensors (Vision, Lidar) collect raw data from the environment.

Example: A robot 'seeing' a wall.

2. Analysis

AI processes raw data to detect patterns or anomalies.

Example: Deciding to turn left.

3. Action

Actuators execute physical tasks based on AI decisions.

Example: Wheels moving forward.
